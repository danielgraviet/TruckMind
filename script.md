---
TruckMind Demo Script (~3:00)

---
[0:00–0:20] Hook

▎ "Starting a food truck is a big gamble. You don't know your menu, your customers, or your market until it's too late — and by the time you do, you've already spent the money."

▎ "TruckMind is an agentic system that autonomously operates any business. Today we focused on food trucks — but the engine underneath works for any venture. We give you a full simulation before you spend a dime."

Show the landing page / concept input field.

---
[0:20–0:40] Step 1 — Describe Your Concept

▎ "It starts with one sentence. I want to start a small taco truck where I sell tacos and other Mexican street food. I'll drop a pin on Provo, Utah."

Type the concept. Select city from the map picker. Click "Find Best Strategy."

▎ "The moment you hit go, two agents spin up simultaneously."

---
[0:40–1:15] Step 2 — Silicon Sampling + Strategy Generation

▎ "The first agent pulls real demographic data from the US Census API for Provo — median income, age distribution, price sensitivity, education levels. It uses that data to generate 100 synthetic customers who statistically match the actual population of your target city. We call this Silicon Sampling."

Show personas populating the grid — avatars with names, ages, occupations appearing one by one.

▎ "Each persona has an income bracket, dietary restrictions, a lunch budget, and a backstory grounded in who actually lives near your truck."

▎ "Simultaneously, a second agent is doing market research — generating three distinct business strategies: a value play, a premium concept, and a niche angle. Each with a full menu, pricing rationale, and competitive position."

Show the three strategy cards appearing.

▎ "Something that previously took weeks of market research and $10,000 through firms like Qualtrics — done in minutes, at token cost. This is the new meta."

---
[1:15–1:50] Step 3 — Live Market Simulation

▎ "Now both outputs come together. Each strategy gets tested against the same 100 personas. Watch the reaction board."

Show the reaction board animating — sentiment dots lighting up, colors shifting per strategy.

▎ "We can see real-time reactions — who's interested, who's skeptical, who'd never show up. The system tracks interest rate, projected daily revenue, average sentiment, and margin across all three."

[PAUSE DEMO — let audience absorb the board]

▎ "The evaluator agent scores all three strategies and picks the winner automatically. No human in the loop. You can still flip between strategies here to see exactly how each persona reacted — but the agent has already done the analysis."

Highlight the winning strategy card. Click through the other strategy tabs to show per-strategy reactions.

---
[1:50–2:20] Step 4 — Launch

▎ "Once the evaluator picks a winner, TruckMind proceeds on its own. You have a 20-second window to review — or you can launch immediately."

Show the countdown bar. Click "Launch now →."

App transitions to the shop dashboard.

▎ "This is your operations center. Starting inventory loaded, cash on hand, AI cashier ready. The business is live."

---
[2:20–3:00] Step 5 — Autonomous Operations

▎ "Now we want TruckMind to run the show. Let's simulate a lunch rush."

Click "Simulate Rush." Orders flood in. Revenue counter animates upward.

▎ "Watch the action feed. The agent isn't just taking orders — it's running the business."

Point to the ActionFeed as entries appear:

▎ "Street tacos just hit a surge — automatic price adjustment. Horchata inventory is low — restock decision made. The al pastor burrito hasn't moved — discount applied. Every one of these is an autonomous decision made by the agent in real time."

Show inventory bars draining, then replenishing.

▎ "In a real production system, that restock trigger doesn't just log a note — it schedules a delivery order from Costco or Walmart. The agent sees the problem and solves it end to end, without you."

▎ "That's TruckMind. From a single sentence to a simulated, fully operating business — in under three minutes. The same engine works for any business. We just started with food trucks."
