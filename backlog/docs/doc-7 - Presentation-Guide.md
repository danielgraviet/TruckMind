# Presentation Guide

## Scoring Reminder
| Criteria | Weight | Our Play |
|----------|--------|----------|
| **Autonomy** | **40%** | Hammer the number: "X autonomous decisions, zero human input." ActionFeed is the proof. |
| **Value** | **30%** | "Founders waste weeks guessing. TruckMind validates in minutes." Real Census data = real grounding. |
| **Technical** | **20%** | Silicon Sampling, two-tier simulation, multi-agent pipeline. Name them. |
| **Demo** | **10%** | Reaction board is the wow. Rush simulation is the closer. |

## Key Phrases to Use
- "Autonomously operates" — not "assists" or "helps with"
- "Silicon Sampling" — name the technique, make it memorable
- "Grounded in real Census data" — not made up, not random
- "Zero human intervention after concept input"
- "[X] autonomous decisions in [Y] minutes"
- "The AI is the business"

## Key Phrases to Avoid
- "Chatbot" — we are not a chatbot
- "Recommends" or "suggests" — the AI decides, it doesn't suggest
- "Simulated" when talking about the shop — the shop operations are real decisions
- "Demo" when describing the product — it's a working system, not a demo

## Moments to Narrate (Don't Let Judges Miss These)
1. **Strategy generation** — "The AI just designed an entire business in 10 seconds"
2. **Persona cards flipping** — "Each of these 300 people is reacting independently"
3. **Tier 2 flood** — "250 personas scored instantly, zero API calls"
4. **First autonomous action** — "Notice that — the AI just [restocked/raised prices] without being asked"
5. **Rush climax** — "Watch the ActionFeed — every one of those is an autonomous decision"
6. **The number** — End with the count. Make it concrete.

## Q&A Prep

**"How is this different from ChatGPT?"**
> ChatGPT answers questions. TruckMind makes decisions. It doesn't wait to be asked — it monitors inventory, adjusts pricing, and restocks autonomously. It's an operator, not an assistant.

**"Is the data real?"**
> The Census data is real — pulled from the US Census Bureau's American Community Survey. The personas are synthetic but statistically grounded in that real data. That's the point of Silicon Sampling.

**"How much does it cost to run?"**
> About $0.50 per full pipeline run. The two-tier simulation is the key — 50 personas get full LLM simulation, then we learn the patterns and score 250 more with zero API calls.

**"Could this work for a real business?"**
> The market validation absolutely could. A real food truck owner could input their concept and get instant demographic-grounded feedback. The autonomous operations layer is a proof of concept, but the pricing and inventory logic is based on real food service patterns.

**"What if the AI makes a bad decision?"**
> That's actually the point of the simulation layer. The AI stress-tests its strategy against 300 synthetic customers before the shop even opens. Bad decisions get caught in simulation, not in production. During operations, every autonomous decision is logged with reasoning — full transparency.
